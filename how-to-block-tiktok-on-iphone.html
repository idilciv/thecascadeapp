How to block TikTok on iPhone

The For You Page is engineered to keep you swiping, and the minutes vanish. Here’s how to block TikTok on your iPhone, from Apple’s built-in limits to a lock you can’t tap past.

TikTok’s whole design is a slot machine: every swipe might be the great one. That’s why “one video” turns into an hour, it’s not a willpower problem, it’s a very good algorithm.

Block TikTok the free way, with Screen Time

iOS has a built-in tool called Screen Time. It can put a daily limit on TikTok in a couple of minutes:

  1. Open Settings and tap Screen Time. If it’s off, tap Turn On Screen Time.
  2. Tap App Limits, then Add Limit.
  3. Search for TikTok, tick it, and tap Next.
  4. Set the limit as low as it goes, 1 minute, then tap Add.
  5. From now on, TikTok greys out once you pass that minute for the day.

For a stricter version, use Downtime (Settings › Screen Time › Downtime) to switch off TikTok during set hours, like work or bedtime.

The catch: it’s one tap to ignore

When the limit hits, iOS still shows an “Ignore Limit” button. One tap, “Ignore for Today”, and TikTok opens anyway. If the habit is strong, that escape hatch is usually all it takes to undo the block.
